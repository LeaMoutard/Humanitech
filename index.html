Us

We are a group of LSE students exploring the intersection of human rights and technology. We believe that technological tools have an incredible potential to both threaten or protect human rights.

one of our meeting

Our actions

We organize different types of events -from workshops to talks, and invite different practitioners to come and share their experiences with us. We also encourage developing awareness on the topic through writing or spreading blog articles.

February Here's a programme of our first events

This first month will give you a taste of the different ways technology impacts human rights. As you can see we cover a broad range of topics and technological tools. We'll hear about social media, machine learning and genome editing, crossing these themes with reflections on gender empowerment and reporting of atrocities. Consider all events as informal conversations in which everyone can participate!

Coming soon War and Tech

With the proliferation of wars around the world, and the increasing threat of mass destruction and civilian casualties caused by advances of technology, it's more important than ever to be aware of what this means for humanity and human rights. On Monday 25th March, we will be exploring innovative perspectives on the protective as well as the destructive functions of technology in war, through small-group conversations with 2 leaders in the field: one focused on preventing civilian deaths in Syria, the other campaigning about the risks posed by so-called "killer robots" and other war tech. The format of the event will be an interactive and intimate discussion/debate between the speakers and attendees - you don't need to have experience in technology or human rights, just bring your curiosity!
